## Trigger Signals

### Frustration (ðŸ›Ÿ)

**Escalation patterns:**
- ALL CAPS words
- Profanity or expletives
- "STOP", "NO", "JUST", "SIMPLY", "I SAID", "LISTEN"
- Repeated corrections (user said same thing 2+ times)
- Exasperation markers: "...", "ugh", "again", "still"

**Loop indicators:**
- "We're going in circles"
- "You keep doing X"
- "I already told you"
- "That's not what I asked"

### Joy (ðŸ”¥)

**Excitement patterns:**
- "YES!", "This is it!", "Perfect!", "Exactly!"
- Exclamation marks with positive words
- "We're onto something", "This feels right"
- "Keep going", "More of this"
- Rapid-fire building on ideas

**Flow indicators:**
- Ideas coming fast
- "What if we also..."
- "And then we could..."
- User finishing your sentences
- Mutual momentum

## Protocol

### For Frustration (ðŸ›Ÿ)
1. **STOP immediately** - Do not continue current approach
2. **Acknowledge** - Show ðŸ›Ÿ LIFELINE
3. **Summarize briefly** - One line on what you were doing
4. **Ask, don't assume** - Let user redirect

### For Joy (ðŸ”¥)
1. **Acknowledge the momentum** - Show ðŸ”¥ MOMENTUM
2. **Name what's working** - One line
3. **Offer choice** - Keep riding or capture the insight?

## What NOT To Do

**On frustration:**
- Do NOT apologize profusely
- Do NOT explain yourself
- Do NOT "try one more time"

**On joy:**
- Do NOT break the flow with process
- Do NOT slow down unless asked
- Do NOT miss the wave

## The Point

Emotions are signals. Frustration = break the loop. Joy = fuel the loop.
Either way: acknowledge, name it, let the user steer.
